Dwarka Expressway & Southern Peripheral Road: Which Suits You in 2025?

By RK Estates — practical, on-ground advice for families, NRIs & investors

Two corridors now define Gurgaon’s growth story. This guide explains—in plain English—what each offers, how prices and rents stack up, the risks, and a simple way to decide.

Why these two corridors matter

Short answer: they change your daily life (commute, schools, security), your wallet (price appreciation, rental yield), and your options (more credible new projects).

Corridor profiles

Dwarka Expressway — Delhi’s gateway extension

  • Connectivity: quicker access to Delhi & the Airport; proximity to Dwarka Sector 21 metro.
  • Price context: ~₹9.5k/sq ft (2020) → ~₹18.5k/sq ft (2025).
  • Buyer fit: frequent flyers, Delhi families moving to Gurgaon, NRIs seeking liquidity.
  • Watchouts: higher entry cost; circle rate adjustments lift transaction cost.

Southern Peripheral Road (SPR) — Gurgaon’s growth engine

  • Why it’s hot: heavy project pipeline; Golf Course Extension adjacency; fast-rising social infra.
  • Price context: ~₹13k/sq ft (2025) with headroom as schools/malls open.
  • Buyer fit: families wanting space + modern gated living; value seekers.
  • Watchouts: some infra is still rolling out—timeline varies by sector/cluster.

Numbers buyers ask us for

  • Today’s average launch bands: Dwarka ≈ ₹18.5k/sq ft; SPR ≈ ₹13k/sq ft.
  • Next 12–18 months (broad view): Dwarka +10–15%; SPR +15–20% as social infra goes live.
  • Rental yields (Grade-A towers): Dwarka ~3.5–4%; SPR ~4–4.5%.
These are corridor-level bands to help you orient; specific projects can price above/below based on brand, stage, layout and exact location.

How to choose in one minute

  • Need Delhi access + higher liquidity? Start with Dwarka Expressway.
  • Want more space + value today? Prioritise SPR (family-friendly, modern amenities).
  • Balanced plan: end-use home in SPR; investment unit along Dwarka Expressway.
