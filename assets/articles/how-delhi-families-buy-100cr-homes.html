Insights • NCR • September 2025

Ever wondered how Delhi families actually buy ₹50–100 Cr homes?

Headlines show the price. Real deals are built on structure: family decisions, financing, and timing. Here’s a clear, practical walkthrough of how high-ticket purchases really close in Delhi.

When a ₹70 Cr bungalow in Shanti Niketan or a ₹90 Cr penthouse on Golf Course Road trades hands, most people ask: “Who is buying this—and how are they paying for it?” The honest answer is: very few write a single cheque. Delhi’s largest deals are mosaics—layered funding assembled to be fast, compliant, and low-friction for both sides.

What really sits behind the cheque

Buyers assemble a capital stack—multiple sources used together. The pieces that appear most often in 2025:

  • Family settlements & inheritance – rebalancing family assets to create purchase capital.
  • Loan-Against-Securities (LAS) – pledging listed portfolios for a quick overdraft to lock the deal.
  • Promoter liquidity lines – share-pledge credit lines that move in days.
  • Private credit / NBFC bridge – short-tenor, property-backed funding when timing is tight.
  • NRI remittances – official NRE/NRO flows under RBI/FEMA, often paired with local leverage.
  • Bank loans & declared savings – on-book funding remains part of the mix even at high tickets.
  • Asset swaps – selling commercial/industrial assets to buy legacy homes.
RBI / FEMA BASICS
  • Payments must flow through banking channels (NRE/NRO/FCNR). No cash/travel cards.
  • Keep proofs: inward remittance, KYC, and bank advice—these matter at resale/repatriation.
  • Coordinate registry timelines with remittance arrival and bank legal vetting.

Two real-world playbooks

1) The 90-day bridge (speed beats price)

A Delhi family pledged a large listed portfolio and drew a 90-day OD. That locked a South Delhi floor in weeks. Proceeds from a warehouse sale cleared the line before maturity. They won against a higher bidder who couldn’t evidence funds fast enough.

2) The NRI split-buy (one decision, two homes)

Two siblings abroad wanted independent homes close to parents. Family settlement + phased remittances + a small LAP against another city asset = synchronized registries within weeks, zero friction.

Buyer playbook: look financially “ready”

  • Line up a proof-of-funds pack: LAS sanction / bank line, banker contact, escrow readiness.
  • Match timelines: remittances, portfolio pledge, private-credit paperwork, registry slot.
  • Use bridge + refinance: lock first, repay from later asset sale to avoid missing the house.

Seller playbook: separate signal from noise

  • Ask for a sanction letter or banker intro, not just a promise.
  • Prefer buyers offering escrow or staged payments aligned to diligence milestones.
  • Set a tight but realistic calendar: token → legal → registry. Certainty is worth price.

Common trip-ups (and how to avoid them)

  • LAS margin calls: keep a buffer; don’t run your line at 99% in a volatile week.
  • Remittance delays: initiate early, reconcile SWIFT refs, and book registry after funds land.
  • Title surprises: align lawyers early; don’t discover society/utility issues on registry day.

The bigger picture

Premium housing demand is being driven less by speculation and more by structured, multi-source capital. Understanding this is the difference between hoping a transaction closes and engineering it to close.
